I am a PhD Candidate at the University of Washington in the Department of Mechanical Engineering, advised by advised by [Dr. Kat Steele](https://steelelab.me.uw.edu/). I am fascinated by movement, motivated by finding patterns, and curious about how individual differences impact performance. I am interested in developing and implementing computational tools to support personalized clinical care. Given my experience in teaching dynamics and biomechanics, I am eager to incorporate education and training for these tools across disciplines.

My doctoral research leverages data science and machine learning to understand patient-specific gait patterns for children with cerebral palsy. I work closely with [Dr. Michael Schwartz](https://www.gillettechildrens.org/care-team/michael-schwartz-phd-clinical-scientist) and the gait analysis team at Gillette Children's in St. Paul, Minnesota to ensure meaningful translation of this work. I also completed an internship at Gillette to gain exposure to patient care and understand the impacts of engineering in clinical settings. There, I developed methods to predict outcomes of gait assessments, comparing frequentist, Bayesian, and neural network approaches. 

When not tied to work, I enjoy exploring new places, playing guitar, and trying crafts that scratch the itch of working with my hands.
